Mobile Data: Personal Weekly Activity Behaviors Analysis

Can mobile data reflect the activity bahaviors? The following visualizations illustrate different aspects of the activity behaviors in a typical week. The dataset was collected using the AWARE framework and was collected from 2018-02-19 to 2018-02-28.

Where are my usual haunts?

GPS coordinates were collected every 180 seconds and mapped to the following heatmap. Through looking at the heatmap, we can easily find spots where I spent most of the time. For instance, we can easily identify that one of the regular haunts is the intersection of Fifth Avenue and Craft Avenue. In fact, this happens to be the home address. Another regular haunt is the School of Fine Art building. Interestingly, based on the heatmap, I spent more time in the CFA building than in the GHC building, which is a little bit unsual for a SCS student. This is because I am also a musican and usually spend more than three hours practicing piano in CFA building on daily basis.

Feel free to look at other regular haunts of mine by dragging or zooming the map!

What does my activity behaviors look like?

The activities data were collected using the Activity Recognition Plugin in the Aware Platform. The information was collected every 60 seconds. To analyze the activity behaviors, the data colelcted by the Activity Recognition were aggregated every other hour. Specifically, "Active Score" was calculated every other hour based on the activities recognized by the Plugin. For every "stationary","automotive","walking","running", and "cycling" activity, 10 points, 20 points, 40 points, 80 points, and 60 points were scored respectively. Then, the Active Socre for each hour was computed by averaging the sum of points over total number of data points within one hour.

What is my most active hours of the day in a typical week?

Despite missing data for certain hours, we can still see the pattern about the active level. I am the most active on Sunday and Saturday and the least active on Monday. In a typical day, I am most active from the noon till 10:pm. Interestingly, on Wednesday and Friday, my active levels started to be high at 11 am in the morning. This also agrees with my regular weekly schedule, as I have morning classes at 10:30 am on Wednesdays and Fridays.

Does weather condition affect my active level?

Sometimes weather condition affects individual's activities. Is this true for me based on the mobile data? Weather conditions were collected for the same period of time using the Open Weather Plugin in the Aware framework. The weather conditions were aggregated for every other hour. Specifically, the average humidity, pressure and wind speed were calculated for each hourly slot. The mode of the weather description for each hourly slot was taked as the descritpion for the entire hour. For instance, for one hourly slot, if the following weather conditions were recorded: "rain","rain","mist", "rain", then the weather "rain" will be considered as the overal weather condition for this particular time frame.

Based on the chart above, it seems that the pressure and wind speed do not strongly correlate with active levels. There is a spike of active scores when the humidity is around 90%. However, other than this spike, no strong correlation or relationship are observed.

Based on the bar chart above, I was most active when the weather condition was "few clouds" and least active when the condition was "overcast clouds". However, the differences among different conditions were not very far apart.

What is my battery usage pattern?

The battery usage pattern of an individual could tell interesting stories about an individual, as smart phones have become important roles in our lives nowadays. The following diagram shows how my battery percentage level changed over a typical week. I usually turned off the phone at night so the missing data reflects my sleeping hours. For instance, on could observe that I probably went to sleep at 1:00am on Monday and woke up at 8:00 am, as these were the earliest and latest available data points recorded. Interestingly, on Saturday, the battery level was almost always full. This also agrees with my habits as I always stay at home on Saturdays and have my phone connected to power sources.
